Microneedling

Microneedling uses fine, sterile needles to create controlled micro-injuries in the skin, think of it as prompting your skin to heal itself stronger. Those tiny channels reach into the epidermis and dermis (the depth adjusted to your specific concern), signaling your body to ramp up its own collagen production and repair the treated area from within.

What It Can Improve

With consistent sessions, microneedling can visibly improve:

  • Boxcar and rolling acne scars
  • Fine lines
  • Overall skin texture
  • Pigmentation
  • Visible signs of aging
  • Scalp and hair concerns

Enhancing Your Results

For clients seeking more dramatic collagen stimulation, microneedling can be combined with PRP in a single session, often called the "Vampire Facial." By delivering your own platelet-rich plasma directly into the micro-channels created during microneedling, this combination amplifies collagen induction beyond what either treatment achieves alone, a popular choice for acne scarring, overall skin rejuvenation, and more pronounced anti-aging results. (See our PRP page for more on how this works.)

Microneedling can also be paired with targeted serums, chosen for your specific skin concerns, for smoother, firmer results, and is available for both facial and scalp treatment, for clients addressing hair thinning.

Before Your Treatment

Stop using retinol or other exfoliating treatments in the days leading up to your appointment (the team will give you a specific timeline).

Who Should Avoid Microneedling

  • Active or severe acne (papular or pustular)
  • Active herpes outbreak
  • Local skin infection
  • Currently taking oral retinoids

During Treatment

Numbing cream is optional and depends on your comfort level and the treatment depth needed. Our team will walk you through this at your consultation.

After Treatment

Expect mild redness and light flaking for a day or two; this is a normal part of the healing process. Strict sun protection is required for at least 48 hours afterward to protect the newly treated skin.
