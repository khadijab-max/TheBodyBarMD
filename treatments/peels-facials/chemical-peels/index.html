Chemical Peels

Chemical peels use a topical solution to exfoliate the top layer of dead skin cells and stimulate new skin cell growth, improving fine lines, acne scarring, hyperpigmentation, and other skin concerns.

What It Treats

  • Aging and sun damage
  • Acne and congestion
  • Acne scarring
  • Pigmentation
  • Uneven skin tone and texture
  • Fine lines and wrinkles
  • Melasma (hormonal pigmentation): Melasma can respond well to peels but is also prone to rebound or worsening with sun exposure, especially in deeper skin tones; your provider will help determine the safest approach for your skin

Common Peel Types

  • Glycolic acid: mild peel, gentle exfoliation, minimal downtime
  • Salicylic acid: targets acne and congestion, with minimal downtime
  • Lactic acid: mild, hydrating peel, minimal downtime
  • Combination: tailored to your specific skin, using a single peel or a combination of epidermal to mid-epidermal depth peels based on your skin condition and goals

How It's Done

  • Skin is cleansed and prepped before the peel solution is applied
  • The solution is left on for a set time based on peel type and depth, then neutralized or removed
  • An anesthetic is generally not required
  • Procedure time is typically 30–60 minutes

Who Should Avoid Chemical Peels

  • Active skin infections, open wounds, or active cold sores at the treatment site
  • Pregnancy or breastfeeding (certain peel types only, confirm with your provider)
  • Known allergy to peeling ingredients
  • Recently tanned or sunburned skin

What to Expect After Treatment

  • Mild redness and flaking are common and typically resolve within a few days for lighter peels
  • Strict sun protection is required during recovery
  • Avoid picking or scratching treated skin

Treatment Summary

  • Downtime: Minimal to moderate, depending on peel strength
  • Full results: 1–4 weeks, depending on peel type
