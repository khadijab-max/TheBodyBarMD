PRP: Regeneration From Your Own Blood

Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP) is one of the most well-studied regenerative treatments available today, using your own blood to stimulate skin rejuvenation, reduce fine lines, and restore hair growth naturally, without foreign substances.

How It Works

Your blood contains platelets packed with growth factor proteins that signal your body to build new collagen, repair tissue, and grow new blood vessels. PRP concentrates these platelets from your own blood and reintroduces them directly where you need regeneration most.

At The Body Bar MD, the process is straightforward:

  • A topical numbing cream is applied to the treatment area for 40–45 minutes
  • While it takes effect, a small blood sample is drawn and spun in a centrifuge to isolate the platelet-rich plasma
  • The PRP is then delivered into the skin or scalp using fine needles, or combined with microneedling for deeper penetration
  • When paired with microneedling, the controlled micro-injury further stimulates collagen production, amplifying PRP's natural effects

Even with numbing, most clients feel some mild sensation during treatment; this is normal and temporary.

Who Benefits From PRP

For hair restoration:

  • Telogen effluvium (acute hair shedding)
  • Postpartum hair loss
  • Hair thinning following illness
  • Generalized hair thinning

For skin:

  • Fine lines and early signs of aging
  • Overall skin rejuvenation, especially combined with microneedling
  • Firmer, more refreshed skin through natural collagen induction
  • Under-eye rejuvenation, softening fine lines and dark circles (bruising and redness may be slightly more noticeable here due to thinner skin)

Before Treatment

  • Avoid blood thinners such as aspirin beforehand
  • Clients with a known bleeding disorder are not candidates for PRP

Who Should Avoid PRP

  • Pregnancy
  • Active localized skin infection at the treatment site (e.g., folliculitis, herpes)
  • Severe seborrheic dermatitis

What to Expect

Minor bruising and mild soreness at injection or microneedling sites are common and typically resolve within a few days. Most clients need 3–4 sessions, spaced several weeks apart, to see full results. Collagen remodeling builds gradually rather than overnight.
