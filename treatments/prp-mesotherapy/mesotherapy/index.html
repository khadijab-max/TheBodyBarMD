Mesotherapy

Mesotherapy is a precision treatment that uses fine meso-needles to deliver targeted serums directly into the skin's middle layer, the mesoderm, where they can act directly on the cells responsible for hydration, tone, and repair, rather than sitting on the surface like a topical product.

What It Treats

  • Skin pigmentation
  • Uneven skin tone
  • Signs of aging
  • Hair and scalp concerns

How the Treatment Works

  • The treatment area is cleansed and numbed with a topical cream, applied 45 minutes before the procedure
  • Fine meso-needles deliver micro-injections roughly one centimeter apart across the treatment area, targeting the skin's middle layer
  • Serums are selected and customized to your specific concerns, whether that's pigmentation, hydration, aging, or hair thinning
  • Because the injections are superficial and use very fine needles, discomfort is generally minimal, though this varies by individual
  • Sessions typically take 30 minutes to an hour

Who Should Avoid Mesotherapy

  • Clients on blood thinners (your provider will advise when to pause these beforehand)
  • Active bleeding disorder
  • Active skin infection
  • Pregnancy

What to Expect

Redness, bruising, and mild swelling at the injection sites are common and typically resolve within a few days. Results build gradually, usually becoming visible within a few weeks as your skin responds to the treatment.

Treatment Schedule

Most clients see optimal results with 3–4 sessions, spaced 3–4 weeks apart.
