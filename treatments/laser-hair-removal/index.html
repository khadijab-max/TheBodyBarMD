Laser Hair Removal

Using the Candela GentleMax Pro Plus, our laser hair removal is designed to work on all skin and hair types, delivering long-term, permanent hair reduction with minimal downtime.

Our Technology

  • The GentleMax Pro Plus is suitable for all skin types and hair types
  • Built-in cryogen cooling keeps the treatment comfortable for most clients
  • Most clients achieve permanent hair reduction in approximately 4–5 sessions, with occasional maintenance sessions afterward

How It Works

The laser targets melanin, the pigment in your hair, and the energy travels down into the mid-to-deep dermis. The heat generated at the hair shaft damages the follicle and its stem cells, reducing the hair's ability to regrow over time.

Before Your Treatment

  • Avoid sun exposure and tanning for 4–6 weeks beforehand
  • Skip waxing, tweezing, threading, or bleaching for 4–6 weeks before your appointment (shaving is fine)
  • Shave the treatment area 24 hours before your appointment
  • Stop retinoids, exfoliants, and other irritating skincare 3–5 days before treatment
  • Let your provider know if you have a history of cold sores or take photosensitizing medications
  • Recently tanned skin can't be treated, due to increased risk of pigmentation changes

Who Should Avoid Laser Hair Removal

  • Pregnancy
  • Active infection at the treatment site
  • Currently taking oral retinoids, or a history of photodermatosis

During Your Treatment

Your treatment area is cleansed beforehand, and protective eyewear is provided. Advanced cooling technology keeps you comfortable throughout the session.

After Your Treatment

  • Mild redness is normal and usually fades within a few hours to 2–3 days
  • Wear SPF 30+ daily and avoid direct sun exposure
  • Skip hot baths, saunas, strenuous exercise, and excess heat for 24 hours
  • No waxing, tweezing, or threading between sessions — shaving is fine
  • Some hair shedding 1–2 weeks after treatment is normal
  • Treating underarms: skip deodorant for 24 hours if any irritation occurs

Most clients return every 4–8 weeks, depending on the treatment area, until the full course is complete.
