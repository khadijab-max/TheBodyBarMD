Botox (Wrinkle Reduction)

Botox relaxes targeted facial muscles to soften the lines and wrinkles caused by repeated movement, while also treating select medical concerns like excessive sweating and teeth grinding.

How It Works

Botox works by temporarily blocking the nerve signals that tell a muscle to contract; without that signal, the muscle relaxes, and the skin above it smooths out. Most clients see results within 3–7 days, with full effect visible by about two weeks. Results typically last 3–4 months before a touch-up is needed.

Treatment Areas & Concerns Treated

  • Crow's feet (lateral canthal lines, around the eyes)
  • Frown lines and forehead lines
  • Gummy smile
  • Bunny lines (scrunch lines of the nose)
  • Smoker's lines (vertical lines around the lips)
  • Oral commissures (downward pull of the mouth corners)
  • Platysma bands (neck)
  • Jawline and chin (chin crinkling)
  • Masseter muscles (jaw slimming or teeth grinding/bruxism)

Mesobotox

A micro-dilute Botox technique is injected superficially across the skin, rather than into individual muscles. It helps refine the appearance of enlarged pores, improves overall skin texture, and softens fine lines for a smoother, more even skin surface.

Botox for Hyperhidrosis

For excessive sweating (hyperhidrosis) of the hands, feet, or underarms, Botox works by blocking the nerve signals that activate sweat glands in the treated area, which is a helpful option for clients whose sweating isn't well controlled by antiperspirants alone.

Who Should Avoid Botox

  • Neuromuscular disorders
  • Pregnancy
  • Active bleeding disorders or blood thinners
  • Active skin infection at the treatment site

After Treatment

  • Remain upright for approximately 6 hours post-treatment
  • Avoid exercise, hot baths, and saunas
  • Avoid facial treatments or massage in the treated area for the remainder of the day

Treatment Schedule

Most clients return every 3–4 months to maintain results, though this varies by treatment area and individual muscle response.
