Vascular & Redness Treatment (Laser)

For spider veins, cherry angiomas, and persistent facial redness, our laser treatment targets abnormal blood vessels beneath the skin, directly reducing redness and visible vessels with minimal downtime.

What It Treats

  • Spider angiomas
  • Cherry angiomas
  • Leg veins
  • Telangiectasias (visible "spider" vessels)
  • Redness associated with rosacea

How It Works

The laser targets oxyhemoglobin, the pigment in your blood, converting light energy into heat within the vessel. That heat causes the vessel wall to collapse and seal shut, so your body naturally reabsorbs it over time, fading the redness or visible vessel.

Before Your Treatment

Avoid blood-thinning medications and supplements for at least one week before treatment. When medically appropriate, always talk to your provider before stopping any medication.

What Happens During Treatment

It's normal to see the treated vessel darken or turn a blue-gray color immediately afterward. This is a sign the treatment is working, not a concern. Some superficial vessels may visibly disappear right away. Mild redness and slight swelling around the treated area are expected and temporary.

Who Should Avoid This Treatment

  • Active papulopustular rosacea (active breakouts or pustules)
  • Active skin infections

After Your Treatment

Avoid strenuous exercise, blood-thinning substances, and activities that raise blood pressure or trigger flushing for at least one week (for facial treatment).

Treatment Schedule

  • Facial vessels: typically, 3 sessions, about a month apart
  • Leg vessels: up to 3 sessions, spaced 8–12 weeks apart. Leg vessels heal more slowly, so full results can take up to 12 weeks to appear.
