Benign Pigmented Lesion Treatment (Brown Spots)

For sunspots, birthmarks, and other benign areas of uneven pigmentation, our laser treatment targets melanin directly, offering a clean, controlled approach to fading brown spots and improving skin tone.

What It Treats

  • Photopigmentation (sun-induced brown spots)
  • Café-au-lait macules
  • Epidermal melasma
  • Epidermal nevi and certain birthmarks

Note: melasma, café-au-lait macules, and epidermal nevi can respond differently from person to person, and some pigmentation may return over time or require maintenance treatment. Your provider will assess your specific case during consultation to set realistic expectations.

How It Works

The laser targets melanosomes, tiny melanin-containing structures within your skin's outer layer. Laser light is absorbed by that melanin, generating heat that breaks down the pigmented cells while leaving the surrounding skin largely undisturbed.

What to Expect During Treatment

  • Mild redness around the treated area
  • The pigmented spot will visibly darken. This is the desired response, a sign the treatment is working, not a concern

After Your Treatment

  • Light crusting develops over the treated area, typically resolving within 1–2 weeks
  • Pigment gradually fades as the treated cells shed naturally

Treatment Schedule

Most clients need 3 sessions, spaced 4–8 weeks apart, for optimal results.
